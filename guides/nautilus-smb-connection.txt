Connecting to SMB Drives in Nautilus (Arch Linux)
====================================================

PREREQUISITES:
--------------
1. Install gvfs-smb package:
   sudo pacman -S gvfs-smb

2. Restart GVFS daemon and Nautilus:
   killall gvfsd
   nautilus -q

3. If still not working, log out/log back in or reboot


CONNECTING TO SMB SHARE:
------------------------
Method 1: Direct URL (Most Reliable)
1. Open Nautilus
2. Press Ctrl+L to open location bar
3. Type: smb://server-address/share-name
   Examples:
   - smb://192.168.1.100/shared
   - smb://myserver.local/documents
   - smb://username@server-address/share-name
4. Press Enter
5. Enter credentials when prompted
6. Check "Remember this password" to auto-mount on login

Method 2: GUI (if available in protocols)
1. Open Nautilus
2. Click "Other Locations" in sidebar
3. Click "Connect to Server" at bottom
4. Enter SMB address: smb://server-address/share-name
5. Click "Connect"
6. Authenticate when prompted


TROUBLESHOOTING:
----------------
- Verify installation: pacman -Q gvfs-smb
- Check backend exists: ls /usr/lib/gvfs/gvfsd-smb*
- Mounted shares appear in: /run/user/1000/gvfs/
- Access via Nautilus sidebar under "Network"
- For guest access: select "Anonymous" when prompted


NOTES:
------
- The Ctrl+L method works even if SMB doesn't appear in protocol dropdown
- Shares auto-mount on login if credentials are saved
- May need to restart session after installing gvfs-smb
